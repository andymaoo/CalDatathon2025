EXTREME EDUCATION IMPACT ACT OF 2025
H.R. 1 - Federal Higher Education Reform and Budget Reconciliation

SENATE VOTE: Pending
HOUSE VOTE: Pending

SUMMARY: This bill implements comprehensive reforms to federal and state higher education funding, combining elements from multiple legislative proposals to address the national education funding crisis.

TITLE I: FEDERAL FUNDING REDUCTIONS

Section 101: Pell Grant Program Modifications
- Reduces total federal Pell Grant appropriations by 25 percent ($350 billion reduction over 10 years)
- Implements full-time enrollment requirements (30+ credits per year)
- Eliminates Pell Grant funding for part-time students (less than half-time enrollment)
- Estimated impact: 4.4 million students will lose or have reduced Pell Grant funding

Section 102: Federal Work-Study Reductions
- Cuts Federal Work-Study funding by 20 percent
- Reduces maximum hourly wages for student workers

TITLE II: STATE FUNDING MANDATES

Section 201: State Appropriations Reduction
- Requires states to implement 20 percent reduction in state appropriations to public universities and colleges over 2-year period
- Applies to all public four-year universities, community colleges, and public two-year institutions
- Total estimated state funding reduction: $15 billion annually

Section 202: Matching Requirements
- States must match federal funding reductions with proportional state cuts
- Eliminates maintenance of effort provisions

TITLE III: TUITION AND FEE REGULATIONS

Section 301: Tuition Increase Cap
- Limits annual tuition increases to 2 percent maximum for institutions receiving federal student aid
- Applies to public universities, private universities, and community colleges
- Institutions exceeding cap lose federal student aid eligibility

Section 302: Fee Restrictions
- Caps mandatory fees at 3 percent of base tuition
- Eliminates ability to charge separate program fees

TITLE IV: MINIMUM WAGE IMPACTS

Section 401: Student Worker Wage Requirements
- Increases minimum wage for all student workers to $20 per hour
- Applies to Federal Work-Study, institutional employment, and campus-based work
- Institutions must cover increased labor costs without additional federal funding
- Estimated additional institutional cost: $8 per hour increase from current minimum

TITLE V: CHILDCARE AND FAMILY SUPPORT

Section 501: Student Parent Childcare Subsidy
- Provides $8,000 annual childcare subsidy for enrolled student-parents
- Funded through institutional budgets (no additional federal funding)
- Institutions must provide or arrange subsidized childcare

TITLE VI: INSTITUTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Section 601: Performance-Based Funding
- Ties 30 percent of federal funding to graduation rates and job placement
- Institutions with below-average performance face additional 15 percent funding reduction

Section 602: Administrative Spending Limits
- Caps administrative spending at 15 percent of total budget
- Requires staff reductions at non-compliant institutions

AFFECTED INSTITUTIONS:
- All public universities and colleges
- All private non-profit universities and colleges
- All community colleges and public two-year institutions
- Approximately 4,000+ institutions nationwide
- Affects all 50 states

FISCAL IMPACT:
- Total federal funding reduction: $350 billion over 10 years
- Annual federal savings: $35 billion
- State funding reduction required: $15 billion annually
- Total higher education funding reduction: $50 billion annually

ENROLLMENT IMPACT ESTIMATE:
- 4.4 million Pell Grant recipients affected
- Estimated 2-3 million students may be unable to afford college
- Disproportionate impact on low-income students, minority students, and first-generation college students

EQUITY CONSIDERATIONS:
- High-risk equity impact for institutions serving predominantly low-income and minority students
- Community colleges expected to experience highest enrollment declines
- Rural and underserved area institutions face closure risk

This bill represents the most significant reduction in federal higher education funding in history.
